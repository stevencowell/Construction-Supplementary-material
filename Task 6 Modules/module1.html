Types of Plans and Their Functions

Construction projects use a set of plans, each with a specific purpose.

Construction Plans: The complete collection of all drawings for a project (site, floor, elevation, etc.).
Location Plan: Shows the project's location in relation to its surroundings, including street names and block numbers.
Site Plan: A detailed aerial view of the entire property block. It shows boundary dimensions, setbacks, building position, orientation (North pointer), contour lines, and locations of services like sewer and stormwater. → See example site plan
Floor Plan: A "bird's-eye view" of a building without the roof. It shows room layouts, internal/external dimensions, wall thicknesses, and the location of doors and windows. → See example floor plan
Elevation Plan: An exterior view of one side of the building (e.g., North Elevation). It shows finished appearance, materials, roof pitch, and window/door heights. → See example elevation
Section Plan: A view showing a "slice" through the building. It provides structural details of footings, walls, and the roof frame.
Detail Plan: A magnified drawing of a specific, complex area (like a footing or window connection) to provide clearer instructions on assembly and dimensions.
Environmental Plan: Outlines actions to manage environmental impacts, such as erosion control, waste management, and air/noise quality.

Key Features of Plans

  • Title Block: Located at the bottom right of every drawing, it contains critical information: drawing number, project name, architect/designer name, scale, date, and the **revision number/letter**.
  • Revision/Amendment Register: This tracks all changes made to the plans. It's crucial for ensuring everyone is working from the most current version. If there's a discrepancy, always check the revision register and title block, then report it to your supervisor.
  • Legend: Explains what the various symbols and abbreviations used on the plan mean (e.g., symbols for doors, windows, power points).
  • North Pointer: A symbol (usually an arrow) that shows the orientation of the plan, allowing you to align it with the actual site. → Find north pointer on site plan
  • Notes: Provide extra detail that can't be shown in the drawing itself, such as framing requirements, insulation types, or specific installation instructions.

Specifications

The project specification is a written document that accompanies the plans. It forms part of the legal contract and details:

  • The quality of work to be carried out.
  • The specific materials to be used.
  • How materials are to be installed.

Products like fasteners, coatings, and adhesives are often supplied with their own manufacturer's specifications.

Common Abbreviations & Symbols

  • FFL: Finished Floor Level
  • BK: Brick
  • SWD: Stormwater Drain
  • DPC: Damp-Proof Course
  • You must also be able to identify symbols for features like toilets, showers, basins, doors, and different material types (e.g., timber frame vs. brick).
