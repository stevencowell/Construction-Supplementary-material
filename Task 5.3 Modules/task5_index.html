Overview of Requirements

To be deemed competent, you must successfully complete three assessment sections covering the planning, manufacturing, and assembly of joinery components.

  • Section 1: Written Questioning. This tests your knowledge of safety standards, quality control, joinery tools, timber joints, and assembly procedures for various items like doors, frames, and cupboards.
  • Section 2: Product-Based Methods. You must create detailed work plans for three distinct joinery projects: a multi-panelled door, a door frame and jamb, and a carcass unit (cupboard). Each plan must include a construction sequence, JSA, and materials list. View Work Plan example ‚Üí View JSA example ‚Üí View Materials List example ‚Üí
  • Section 3: Direct Observation. An assessor will observe you planning and building the three projects. You must demonstrate safe work practises, accurate manufacturing, correct assembly techniques, and proper cleanup procedures.

You must achieve a "Satisfactory" result in all parts of all three sections to be judged competent.
